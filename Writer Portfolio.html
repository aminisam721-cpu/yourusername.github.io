"A Day Among the Dates"

Off White, RMIT Gazette 2018
Creative Writing Literary Fiction University Publication

"The market stretched endlessly under the desert sun, rows of dates hanging like amber pendants in the stifling air. Each vendor called out prices in a language that danced between Arabic and commerce, their voices weaving through the maze of covered stalls where time seemed suspended in honey-thick light."

"She moved through the market like a ghost of her former self, touching the smooth skin of dates that reminded her of home—not the home she'd left behind, but the one she'd never quite managed to find. In this space between cultures, between languages, between the person she was and the person she was becoming, the dates offered a sweet familiarity that transcended geography."

"Sounds of Behaviour"

Ordinary Wonders, Bowen Street Press 2017
Literary Fiction Experimental Writing Audio Landscape

"The morning arrived with its usual collection of small violences—the alarm clock's shrill protest, coffee grounds scattered across the counter like tiny accusations, the newspaper's headlines bleeding through the kitchen window's filtered light."

"She listened to the city waking up: garbage trucks grinding their mechanical teeth, car doors slamming shut like punctuation marks ending overnight dreams, the distant hum of early commuters already surrendering to another day's rhythm. Each sound carried its own behavioral signature, a sonic fingerprint of urban existence that she had learned to read like sheet music."
